However, I also believe that it is ultimately up to the responsibility of the website owners and creators that they are fully transparent and more upfront about the use of information on their website. Zucherbergs defense for many of these claims is that they offer ways out, but what he doesnt mention is that it took large backlash from the public in order for that to happen, which brings into question if the people behind Facebook ever consider morality when making these decisions. When websites are committing morally questionable acts, it should be vocalised and taken seriously. If they only react to the public outbursts, than better work needs to be done about informing the viewers of decisions being made, and the options they have in order to opt out if they so desire.
